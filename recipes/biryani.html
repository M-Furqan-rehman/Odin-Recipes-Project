Biryani

Biryani is one of the most famous traditional foods of Pakistan. It is a rice-based dish made with aromatic basmati rice, meat, and a mix of rich spices.
The origins of biryani trace back to the Mughal era, when Persian cooking styles mixed with Indian flavors. Over time, it became a popular festive dish in the Indian subcontinent, especially in Pakistan, where cities like Karachi and Lahore are known for their unique styles of biryani.
Today, it is cooked in almost every household and served at weddings, family gatherings, and special occasions, symbolizing celebration and hospitality.

Ingredients (Chicken Biryani serves people of 4)

  • basmati rice 2cup (soaked)
  • chicken bone-in : 700g
  • onion : 3 large thin sliced
  • tomatoes : 2 choped
  • yogurt : 1 cup
  • oil or ghee : 1/3 cup
  • biryani masala : 2 tbsp
  • ginger and garlic paste : 2tbsp
  • green chilies 3,4 slit
  • mint + cilantro mixed + choped
  • lemon juice : 1 tbsp
  • whole spices : 2 bay leaves,1tsp cumin,4 cardamom,4 cloves,1 small cinamon,8-10 peppercorns
  • salt as tasted

How To Make

  1. Fry onion in oil/ghee until deep golden. Reserved half.
  2. In the same pot,add whole spices,then add ginger-garlic,saute 30 sec.
  3. Add chicken;brown 5-6 min
  4. Add tomatoes,yogurt,biryani masala,chilies,salt;cook until thick and oil separates.Stir in half the herbs.
  5. Boil rice in salted water with a few whole spices until 70% done;drain
  6. Layer: gravy → half rice → fried onions/herbs/lemon → remaining rice → optional saffron milk/kewra. Dot with a little ghee.
  7. Seal pot; steam on low (dum) 20–25 min. Rest 10 min, then fluff and serve.
